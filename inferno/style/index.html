Why It Matters

If it makes my eyes bleed, am I going to be motivated to help?

Attention To Detail Is Important

Programming is a detail oriented art. If you neglect details you will make lots of mistakes, your code will be bad, your programs will have errors, you will cause yourself (and others) much undue pain debugging problems you could have easily avoided.

Caring is important

If you don’t care about something then you won’t do a good job. Your final product will be low quality and bad. Your work will cause pain for you and for others.

Why should you care?

  • If you don’t care about what you eat, can I interest you in this turd sandwich? Maybe a urine cocktail, or a vomit stew?
  • If you don’t care about your privacy, then surely you don’t mind if I watch you take a shower?
  • If you don’t care about your personal belongings, then surely I can simply take them for myself. It’s not stealing, because you don’t care, right?
  • If you don’t care about your code, then surely your code will be bad.

A Story

The parable of The Brown M&M’s is a story about Van Halen, a popular 1980s rock band.

As part of their contract they requested a bowl of M&M’s candy in their dressing room with all the brown colored ones removed.

This seems like a small detail, and it admittedly is, but small details can matter a lot.

Bands like Van Halen and Kiss had (still have?) fireworks, explosions and other pyrotechnics on stage. This is fire. Fire can kill people, it is no laughing matter.

If the venue organizers neglected to observe one of the details in the contract, maybe they also neglected to observe some important safety guideline for working with fire on stage.

Van Halen claims that this in fact the case, that any time they found brown M&M’s, a subsequent stage audit would reveal dangerous flaws and oversights in the stage preparation.

The Moral Of The Story

One seemingly small oversight is often indicative of larger, worse and more dangerous problems.

It’s also indicative of carelessness and low quality work.

Canary In The Coal Mine

If one or more small easily addressable problems are present in your code, then I instantly begin to wonder how many other problems there are with your code.

The answer is: probably a lot.

If it seems apparent that you may have hundreds of other problems that you could have avoided through attention to detail then I may be bit apathetic to help.

Actually Caring

I have spent thousands of hours caring about and perfecting my craft.

If you show me that you probably don’t care about what is not only my hobby, but my career and my life, then I feel sad and I probably don’t want to help you.

When you show me that you do care, at least even a little bit, then I do want to help you.
