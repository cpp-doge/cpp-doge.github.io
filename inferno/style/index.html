A Matter Of Style

What Is Style

Style consists of the subjective choices you make like how you name your variables, how you organize your files, how much whitespace you use and where you put that whitespace.

Why It Matters

If it makes my eyes bleed, am I going to be motivated to help?

Attention To Detail Is Important

Programming is a detail oriented art. If you neglect details you will make lots of mistakes, your code will be bad, your programs will have errors, you will cause yourself (and others) much undue pain debugging problems you could have easily avoided.

Caring is important

If you don’t care about something then you won’t do a good job. Your final product will be low quality and bad. Your work will cause pain for you and for others.

Why should you care?

  • If you don’t care about what you eat, can I interest you in this turd sandwich? Maybe a urine cocktail, or a vomit stew?
  • If you don’t care about your privacy, then surely you don’t mind if I watch you take a shower?
  • If you don’t care about your personal belongings, then surely I can simply take them for myself. It’s not stealing, because you don’t care, right?
  • If you don’t care about your code, then surely your code will be bad.

A Story

The parable of The Brown M&M’s is a story about Van Halen, a popular 1980s rock band.

As part of their contract they requested a bowl of M&M’s candy in their dressing room with all the brown colored ones removed.

This seems like a small detail, and it admittedly is, but small details can matter a lot.

Bands like Van Halen and Kiss had (still have?) fireworks, explosions and other pyrotechnics on stage. This is fire. Fire can kill people, it is no laughing matter.

If the venue organizers neglected to observe one of the details in the contract, maybe they also neglected to observe some important safety guideline for working with fire on stage.

Van Halen claims that this in fact the case, that any time they found brown M&M’s, a subsequent stage audit would reveal dangerous flaws and oversights in the stage preparation.

The Moral Of The Story

One seemingly small oversight is often indicative of larger, worse and more dangerous problems.

It’s also indicative of carelessness and low quality work.

Canary In The Coal Mine

If one or more small easily addressable problems are present in your code, then I instantly begin to wonder how many other problems there are with your code.

The answer is: probably a lot.

If it seems apparent that you may have hundreds of other problems to fix before we really get to helping you with the core of your main problem, then I don’t really want to participate,

I also wonder why you don’t care to fix these.

Actually Caring

If you don’t care enough to format your code nicely so it is pleasant to read for yourself (much less other people) then I don’t care to help you.

I have spent thousands of hours caring about and perfecting my craft. When you show me that you fundamentally probably don’t care about what is effectively not only my hobby, but my career and my life, then I feel rather insulted and I don’t want to help you.

Show me that you care.

What And How

Be Consistent

Consistency breeds familiarity. Familiarity breeds quick recognition. Quick recognition frees your brain to think about more important things. All of this means you can work faster and write better more robust code.

There are many subjective decisions to be made with regard to style.

  • Should you use same line curly braces or next line curly braces?
  • Should you use hungarian notation?
  • Should you put spaces inside of conditional clauses?
  • Camel case, snake case or Pascal case?
  • East const or west const?
  • East ref or west ref?
  • East pointer or west pointer?

The answer is simple:

  • It doesn’t matter what you choose
  • Just keep it the same for everything
  • Keep it consistent

Whitespace

We put spaces between words in English to help make things easier to read.

You should do the same for your code.

Whitespace characters are things like spaces, line breaks and tabs.

These are the “invisible” yet visible characters. They contain no squiggly lines like the letter g or the letter z but they still affect how your code looks.

When you press the space bar you insert a space. When you hit enter you insert a line break, also called a newline.

Where whitespace is allowed, you may use as much of it as you wish.

If you are allowed to put one space, you may instead put 3 or 4, or put a line break instead.

Indentation

We indent code blocks to make it more immediately obvious which code belongs to which blocks.

You should indent your code appropriately. You should also make use of whitespace like newlines. For example

Really Bad

void blarg(){ while(boop){if(foo+1){bing;baz; bar;} }}

This is bad because

  • All the code is on one line.
  • There are very few spaces
  • There are no line breaks
  • The placement of the spaces is inconsistent and makes no logical sense

Bad

void blarg(){
while(boop){if(foo+1){
bing;
baz;
bar;
}
}}

This is bad because

  • The logical sections are not indented
  • There are still very few spaces
  • The placement of the curly braces {}, spaces and line breaks is inconsistent, and makes no logical sense and is difficult to visually reason about

Good

void blarg()
{
    while (boop)
    {
        if (foo + 1)
        {
            bing;
            baz;
            bar;
        }
    }
}

This is good because

  • There is more visual separation
    • It is easier to read for the same reason that we add spaces between words in English
  • It is properly indented
    • It is clear which code is logically grouped together
  • It is easy to see that the { and } all match and line up and are correctly balanced
    • Same line braces are also fine, but they are a bit more difficult to easily and quickly visually match up
  • The usage of whitespace and indentation is consistent, making the code visually predictable
    • Predictable is easier and faster to understand and reason about
